Mask Flex Demand & Lift Gap by Face Width
The critical variable is bizygomatic breadth (BZ) — face width at cheekbone level — not head circumference. Narrow faces demand more total groove flex; when grooves reach their limit, the outer panels lift at the cheeks and temples.
Total Groove Flex Demand vs. Face Width
Degrees of total bend required across all 3 grooves to fully conform the mask to the face. Dashed line = groove capacity (3 × max flex). Gap above line = unmet flex = lift at temple.
Bizygomatic Breadth vs. Mask Wing Conformance Gap
Wing tip gap (mm) as a function of BZ — narrower faces (smaller BZ) have higher curvature, demanding more groove flex and producing larger lift gaps where the flat wing panel separates from the face.
Gap Profile Along Mask Arc — Face Contact to Wing
Gap between mask surface and face at every point from center (nose) to outer edge (wing tip), for each BZ percentile. Solid line = face contact zone (groove flex model). Dotted extension = wing zone beyond the cheekbone, where the face curves posteriorly and the mask lifts away. The transition point shifts rightward on wider faces.
Mask Arc Flex — Population Analysis PRIMARY FIT CONSTRAINT
Two independent gap sources. Groove flex lift = seal-critical cheek gap from insufficient groove bend. Temporal recession = inherent geometric gap as the flat wing panel cannot follow the face curving posteriorly — present for all faces, not a defect.

Weighted Overall Fit
Methodology: Each dimension's fit probability is calculated as the area under the combined male/female normal distribution within your product's min–max range. The overall fit is a weighted combination of per-dimension coverage scores. Weights represent the relative importance of each fit constraint for your product — adjust them to reflect which dimensions are truly limiting.

Population Coverage Summary
★ Bell curves are estimated normal distributions derived from published 5th / 50th / 95th percentile data. Tragus-to-tragus and mastoid-to-mastoid arc values are derived (≈½ cranial circumference each). Bizygomatic breadth is a direct caliper measurement from Zhuang & Bradtmiller (2005) Head-and-Face Anthropometric Survey of U.S. Respirator Users, NIOSH/NPPTL, N=3,997 weighted to U.S. workforce (male 5th/50th/95th = 13.2/14.35/15.5 cm · female = 12.4/13.51/14.6 cm). Other sources: NIOSH 2003 anthropometric survey; FAA AM-93-10 (1993); Tollefson et al. (Plast. & Reconstr. Surg.); German longitudinal ear study (2007). For clinical use consult primary literature.
